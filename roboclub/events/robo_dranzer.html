Introduction

In today’s time in the age of naval robotics when most of the researches are being carried out at water surface or beneath the water there is a great demand of water robots to perform various activities like for research purpose, ammunition, surveying etc. So, according to the need and demand we are bringing this event so as to cultivate the talent of students in the field of naval robotics. As, there is a demand of deep ocean study which cannot be performed by humans, so we can clearly understand the need of naval robotics.

Rules
  1. The competition comprises of only one task. This task will comprise of three stages.
  2. In the first stage the bot (boat) has to pass through a gate and the style points will be awarded according to the way it crosses the gate.
    For ex: A backward entry through the gate.
  3. The time points will be awarded as the bot passes the gate within a stipulated time.
  4. For the Second stage the bot has to reach a ball within a specified time.
  5. The distance between the balls will be 50cm.
  6. If the bot touches any ball, it will be awarded negative points of 10.
  7. In the third and final task there will be an accuracy test. The bot will be given two targets (both balls), one with tick and the other with a cross which are placed at equal distance. The participant would be told to hit a ball, points would be awarded according to the right ball hit.
  8. This task brings the bot towards the end of competition where it has to cross the finishing line.
  9. Points shall be awarded separately for the separate stages as well as time points for the entire task.
Bot Description
  1. The size of the bot should not be more than 30×30×30cm respectively.
  2. The battery voltage should not be above 12v.
  3. The bot should be remote controlled (wired or wireless).
  4. The power source should be placed on the bot itself.
